# Axiom Career Intelligence Strategy

## Product Vision

Axiom should evolve from a CV generator into a career intelligence and professional growth platform: a system that helps people understand where they are, choose where to go, close the gap, prove their value, find opportunities, prepare for them, and keep improving over time.

The goal is not to help users create one document. The goal is to become the career operating system they return to whenever they need clarity, momentum, confidence, or opportunity.

Axiom can serve students, fresh graduates, job seekers, career changers, freelancers, early professionals, and ambitious mid-career users by combining:

- Personalized career diagnosis
- Opportunity discovery
- Skills and learning guidance
- Professional branding
- Interview and assessment preparation
- Accountability systems
- Mentorship and community
- Proof-of-work portfolios
- Labor market intelligence
- Long-term career planning

The strongest version of Axiom is not "Canva for CVs." It is "career GPS plus execution coach."

## Problem-Solving Strategy

Most career platforms focus on one small moment: write a CV, apply to jobs, take a course, or message people. Real users struggle because career growth is fragmented across too many tools and too little guidance.

Axiom should solve the career journey as a connected loop:

1. Diagnose: What is the user's current market position?
2. Decide: What roles, industries, and paths fit them?
3. Build: What skills, projects, proof, and profile assets do they need?
4. Package: How should they present their value across CV, LinkedIn, portfolio, and applications?
5. Apply: Which opportunities fit, and how should they tailor their approach?
6. Prepare: What interviews, tests, tasks, and conversations are coming?
7. Improve: What feedback is the market giving them?
8. Grow: What habits, relationships, and milestones compound over time?

The retention strategy should be based on career momentum, not document storage. Users should return because Axiom has their goals, applications, skill gaps, progress, interviews, networking targets, and weekly action plan.

## Feature Ideas

### 1. Career Health Score

**Problem:** Users do not know how competitive they are in the job market.

**Why users struggle:** They receive vague advice, compare themselves to random people online, and rarely get a structured diagnosis.

**Axiom solution:** Score the user's CV, LinkedIn, skills, experience, portfolio, applications, and interview readiness against target roles. Show a clear health score with specific improvement actions.

**Engagement and retention:** Users return weekly to improve their score and unlock stronger opportunities.

**Differentiation:** Ordinary CV builders stop at formatting. Axiom evaluates market readiness.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Revenue opportunities:** Premium diagnostics, recruiter-reviewed reports, institution dashboards.

### 2. Role Fit Engine

**Problem:** Many users apply to roles that do not match their background or interests.

**Why users struggle:** Job titles are confusing, industries use different language, and people often chase popular roles without understanding fit.

**Axiom solution:** Analyze background, strengths, interests, constraints, salary goals, and market demand to recommend realistic target roles.

**Engagement and retention:** Users revisit recommendations as they gain skills or change goals.

**Differentiation:** Moves Axiom from document creation to career direction.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Revenue opportunities:** Premium career path reports, coaching upsells.

### 3. Skill Gap Analyzer

**Problem:** Users do not know which skills actually block them from better roles.

**Why users struggle:** Course platforms push content, job descriptions are noisy, and users cannot separate must-have skills from nice-to-have skills.

**Axiom solution:** Compare the user's profile to real target roles and identify the top missing skills, evidence gaps, tools, certifications, and project needs.

**Engagement and retention:** Creates a living improvement plan users can check off.

**Differentiation:** Connects skills directly to employability instead of generic learning.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Revenue opportunities:** Affiliate course revenue, paid learning plans, employer-sponsored upskilling.

### 4. Career Roadmap Builder

**Problem:** Users want better careers but do not know the sequence of actions.

**Why users struggle:** Advice is scattered and often unrealistic for their current stage.

**Axiom solution:** Generate 30, 60, 90, and 180-day roadmaps based on target role, availability, current skills, and market conditions.

**Engagement and retention:** Weekly tasks, milestones, reminders, and progress check-ins bring users back.

**Differentiation:** Becomes a long-term planning tool rather than a one-time CV utility.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Revenue opportunities:** Premium roadmap plans, accountability subscriptions.

### 5. Weekly Career Action Plan

**Problem:** Job seekers lose momentum after initial motivation.

**Why users struggle:** Career progress is emotionally draining and lacks structure.

**Axiom solution:** Give users a weekly plan: jobs to apply for, people to contact, skills to practice, profile improvements, and interview drills.

**Engagement and retention:** Natural weekly usage loop.

**Differentiation:** Axiom becomes an execution partner.

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Revenue opportunities:** Subscription tier, SMS or WhatsApp accountability.

### 6. Smart Job Matching

**Problem:** Job boards overwhelm users with irrelevant roles.

**Why users struggle:** Keyword matching ignores transferable skills, career stage, location constraints, and application likelihood.

**Axiom solution:** Match jobs using profile data, career goals, skill evidence, salary preferences, experience level, and growth potential.

**Engagement and retention:** Users check new matches daily or weekly.

**Differentiation:** Axiom understands the user better than generic job boards.

**Difficulty:** Hard

**Revenue opportunities:** Job posting fees, featured employers, premium matching.

### 7. Application Tracker

**Problem:** Users lose track of where they applied and what happened.

**Why users struggle:** Applications happen across job boards, email, referrals, forms, and messaging platforms.

**Axiom solution:** Track applications, statuses, deadlines, contacts, tailored CV versions, interview dates, and follow-up reminders.

**Engagement and retention:** Users return whenever they apply, follow up, or interview.

**Differentiation:** Connects CV creation to the full job search workflow.

**Difficulty:** Easy

**Revenue opportunities:** Premium analytics, job search CRM tier.

### 8. Application Quality Coach

**Problem:** Users apply to many jobs poorly instead of fewer jobs strategically.

**Why users struggle:** They do not know how to tailor CVs, cover letters, answers, and proof points to each role.

**Axiom solution:** Score each application for fit, keyword coverage, evidence strength, and clarity before submission.

**Engagement and retention:** Users use Axiom every time they apply.

**Differentiation:** Axiom improves application outcomes, not just documents.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Revenue opportunities:** Premium application packs, pay-per-review.

### 9. ATS Reality Checker

**Problem:** Users obsess over ATS myths but still submit weak applications.

**Why users struggle:** Online advice is full of fear, misinformation, and templates.

**Axiom solution:** Explain whether the CV is parsable, relevant, specific, and aligned to the job description. Focus on practical fixes rather than fake "ATS hacks."

**Engagement and retention:** Used repeatedly for every important application.

**Differentiation:** Builds trust by being honest and outcome-focused.

**Difficulty:** Medium

**Revenue opportunities:** Premium job-specific optimization.

**Engagement and retention:** Users return as they update their roles, skills, and posts.

Axiom should not compete only on better CV templates. Templates are easy to copy.

That accumulated context becomes Axiom's moat.

The product should make users feel:

That is how Axiom becomes a platform users return to weekly or daily long after they create a CV.
